4/18/20 - Blended topography code with interactive selection code. Thought this would be straight forward, but had some issues with color displaying properly and getting five unique topographies to appear. Luckily I ended up figuring it out.

4/21/20 - Ran the code, imported individual topographies into solidworks, put each layer on its own plane where each plane is 3" apart, placed guide curves through each layer, then lofted to create a solid. It's a little hard to see it, but the topographical image is at the base of the sculpture.

I originally wanted a 5x5 grid where each row displays a new generation, but due to time this was reduced to a 3x3 grid. Nevertheless, the different colored sculpture represents the one that had greatest fitness score of its row and as a result, its genes moved on to the next generation/row. As one can see, the three pieces of the final row are very similar to the scultpures with the greatest fitness score. The materials used in the models are two types of bronze. There are many methods of fabricating these, but die casting or blow molding are probably the best options if I were to create them. As for the size, I really wanted to exhibit the form in an attempt to show the audience that it's what's evolving, while the difference in color gives some indication as to where the succeeding sculptures came from.
